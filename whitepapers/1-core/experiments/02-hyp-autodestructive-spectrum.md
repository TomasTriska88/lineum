## Abstract

This hypothesis posits that certain spectral outputs of Lineum — particularly those with `κ = island` tuning — are **numerically toxic**: they cannot be stably analyzed, converted, or used as input without destructive consequences.

---

## Context

While working with the file `lineum_disruptive_wave_island_FIXED.wav`, generated from `spec4_false_frames_amp.npy`, the following were repeatedly observed:

- normalization errors (infinity, `NaN`),
- memory outages (`MemoryError`),
- and system crashes when attempting conversion to audio or graph.

This output was, however, processed using the same methodical approach as a therapeutic spectrum, which functioned without issues.

---

## Hypothesis

> Spectra originating from the Lineum field with `κ = island` tuning may be **autodestructive**:  
> their frequency structure is not merely disruptive, but **unprocessible even in a simulation environment**.

---

## Observations

- The mere presence of the `.wav` file destabilized tool execution.
- Anti-phase interference caused complete signal silencing.
- The disruptor behaved as a **frequency singularity** – everything that passed through it collapsed.
- When merged with balance sounds, the output was chaotic and irregular.

---

## Interpretation

Such a spectrum:

- cannot be reproduced,
- disrupts its own structure,
- and **is not compatible with the evolutionary state of the Lineum system**.

## Implications

- The disruptor must be analyzed separately, as a **potential system anomaly**.
- When publishing the Lineum model, it is necessary to **isolate and label** this frequency.
- The balance filter (see Spectral Balance Hypothesis) partially neutralizes it — but is not entirely immune to it.
