## 3. Core Mechanics / Theory
There are three distinct theoretical pathways for anti-gravity propulsion in Lineum:

### A. The \u03bc-Field Eraser ("The Snowplow")
Gravity requires a historically carved \u03bc gradient. An active drive could emit highly asymmetric, incoherent noise (manipulating the local \u03ba matrix) directed downwards. This localized entropic burst would temporarily erase or "flatten" the \u03bc field directly beneath the craft. By destroying the local memory of the Earth's gravity well, the \u03a6 field flattens into a plateau. The craft ceases to fall because it is mathematically standing on level ground.

### B. The Gradient Inverter ("The Gravitational Surfer")
Rather than erasing the field, a craft could use a phased array of \u03a8-wave emitters to create constructive interference directly beneath it. By artificially inflating the \u03a6 pressure underneath the hull, the drive creates a localized topological "hill." Since linons naturally drift down the \u03a6 gradient (away from high pressure), the craft constantly slides "downward" from its own artificially generated hill, resulting in net upward motion.

### C. The \u03ba-Phase Cloak ("The Frictionless Hull")
For a linon to experience the Basin Capture Effect, its internal loops must interact with the surrounding \u03a6 gradient. If the hull of the craft were composed of nodes with perfectly tuned dissipation or noise parameters (\u03ba), it could achieve "topological superconductivity." The hull would become perfectly smooth to the underlying spacetime grid, failing to register the \u03a6 gradient altogether. Lacking this interaction "friction," the craft would become inert to gravity and slide freely outward along its geodesic.
