# Lineum: The Multi-Paper Publication Architecture

Given the absolute explosion of profound theoretical breakthroughs, Lineum is structured not as a single file, but as a living ecosystem of documents. 

To maintain scientific credibility and prevent confusion between hard proofs and philosophical theories, the repository is divided into strictly categorized tiers based on their **level of authority and rigor**.

## 📗 Tier 1: Core (`1-core/`)
**Authority:** Absolute. Mathematically locked and contract-validated.
**Description:** The foundational ruleset and numeric proofs. If it is in this folder, it has been executed, compiled, and proven to work within exact tolerance bands.
**Expectation:** Hard math, discrete geometry, and rigorous falsifiability. It avoids any philosophical speculation or macroscopic assertions. Contains `01-lineum-core.md`.

### 🔬 Sub-Tier: Experiments (`1-core/experiments/`)
**Authority:** Empirical Evidence (Observational).
**Description:** Laboratory logs, raw simulation outputs, and edge-case testing that inform the theories but are not part of the primary canon.

## 📘 Tier 2: Cosmology (`2-cosmology/`)
**Authority:** Working Draft / Hypothesis.
**Description:** Macroscopic phenomena (gravity, multiverse topology, relativistic effects). These are logical mathematical extrapolations derived from the Core mechanics, but they are subject to active revision.
**Expectation:** High-level systemic thinking and theoretical physics analogies. Contains `01-lineum-cosmology.md`.

## 📙 Tier 3: Ontology (`3-ontology/`)
**Authority:** Philosophical / Interpretive.
**Description:** Frameworks concerning consciousness, memory entailment, the subjective experience of the observer, and existential cycles (e.g., the $\varphi$ database).
**Expectation:** Cross-disciplinary speculation (cognitive science, theology, systems theory) trying to map human experience to the Lineum geometry.

## 🗄️ Tier 4: Archive & Dead Ends (`4-archive/`)
**Authority:** Retracted / Falsified.
**Description:** In emergent physics, documenting what *doesn't* work is as important as what does. This folder serves as a graveyard for falsified hypotheses, mathematical dead ends, and legacy assumptions that were disproven by later limit scans.
**Expectation:** Historical context. Papers here will be clearly marked with a `Status: Retracted` badge.

---
*Note: The frontend Portal dynamically parses the `Status:` field at the top of each Markdown file to visually render whether a document is a validated `Locked` proof, a working `Draft`, or a `Retracted` dead end.*
